Soha Niroumandi

Soha Niroumandi is a PhD student and Research Scientist at the Medical Flow Physics Laboratory at the University of Southern California, advised by Niema Pahlevan. She also holds an MSc in Computer Science from USC. Her work lies at the intersection of computer science and medical engineering, utilizing her expertise in both fields. In her research, Soha explores how to leverage information from heart-brain-aorta coupling to develop non-invasive techniques for detecting cardiovascular and cerebrovascular diseases.

She recieved the American Heart Association Predoctoral Fellowship for her contrubutions to developing a non-invasive, smartphone-based diagnostic tool for detecting dementia. She is also recipient the Phi Kappa Phi National Honor Society’s Student Recognition Award and selected as MIT Rising Star in Mechanical Engineering 2025.
